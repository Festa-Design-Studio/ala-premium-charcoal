About Atomic Design

Atomic Design is a methodology composed of five distinct stages working together to create interface design systems in a more deliberate and hierarchical manner. The five stages of Atomic Design are:

1. Atoms

The basic building blocks of matter. In interfaces, atoms are our HTML tags, such as form labels, inputs, buttons, and others that can't be broken down any further without ceasing to be functional.

2. Molecules

Groups of atoms bonded together that take on new properties. In interfaces, molecules are relatively simple groups of UI elements functioning together as a unit.

3. Organisms

Groups of molecules joined together to form a relatively complex, distinct section of an interface. Organisms form the sections of our interfaces.

4. Templates

Page-level objects that place components into a layout and articulate the design's underlying content structure. They focus on content structure rather than the content itself.
